Paws of Time

Origins ~7500 BCE

7500 BCE is the earliest known evidence of cat domestication. Archaeologists discovered a burial with a cat's skeleton alongside a human in Shillourokambos, Cyprus. During this time, cats were not native to the island, suggesting that humans intentionally brought them and valued them as companions.

4000 BCE

In ancient Egyptian societies, cats became important companions for protecting crops from rats and other pests, and were also associated with religous figures such as Bastet, the protector goddess. As a common practice at the time, Cats were mummified in as a religious offering to honor and worship the gods.

1500 BCE

By 1500 BCE, the domestication of cats began spreading from Egypt into other parts of the Mediterranean and Asia through trade routes. Cats were predominantly recognized as companions and protectors by sailors and merchants, and they have become established in various regions.

700 CE

At this point, the domestication of cats had reached Japan from China and Korea and became culturally valued as they were often depicted in artworks, folklore and symbolism as protectors and everyday companions.

1871

The world's very first cat show was held at the Crystal Palace in London in 1871. The event marked the rise of selective breeding and its growing international attention. The view of cats has shifted from being originally seen as working animals to having standards for various breeds as pets.

Modern

Today, cats have become trusted companions and are valued as family members in households. Their popularity as pets has pushed advances in veterinary care, nutrition, and breeding. Cats have also played a significant role in pop culture and online culture, through popular memes and references.
